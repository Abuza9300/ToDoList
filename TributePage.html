Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India. He is also known as the Missile Man of India for his work on the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology.

Early Life

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. He graduated in physics and later studied aerospace engineering.

Career

Kalam spent four decades as a scientist and science administrator, mainly at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). He was intimately involved in India's civilian space program and military missile development efforts.

Presidency

He served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. During his term, he was widely referred to as the People's President.

Legacy

Kalam was a recipient of several prestigious awards, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor. He inspired millions with his vision for a developed India and his dedication to education and scientific progress.
